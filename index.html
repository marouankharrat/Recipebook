Tunisian cuisine is a diverse mix of flavors representative of the country's past and central location in North Africa. While cuisine varies regionally, Tunisian food typically combines French and African flavors with a spicy kick. Tunisian dishes featuring couscous are often made with fresh seafood or hearty lamb, depending on local availability. Tunisia is the place of cultures and not only offers local food. Foodies can enjoy all types of international cuisine in the country's major cities. In short, Tunisia is a foodie's paradise. Here are some popular Tunisian foods you must try:

Tunesische Couscous

Couscous is probably the most famous dish from North Africa. Couscous is originally a Berber dish and consists of a stew-like sauce made from vegetables such as potatoes, tomatoes, zucchini, Carrots, or pumpkin and lamb or chicken served on a bed of semolina.
